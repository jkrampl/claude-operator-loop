# Pitch deck template

## Target
B2B pitch / sales deck. Retailer, distributor, investor, partner, event sponsor. 12-18 slides typical. Output as markdown by slide; HTML or pptx if user requests.

## Structure (standard 14-slide format)

### Slide 1 — Cover
- Brand name + tagline
- Decks for: "for [Recipient name / company]"
- Date + deck author

### Slide 2 — Hook / opener
- One strong statement, specific
- Either a big number (with source) or a provocative question
- Sets the frame for the whole deck

### Slide 3 — About us (1 slide, not more)
- Company at a glance (3-5 bullets)
- Legal entity / year founded / location
- Credentials most relevant to THIS recipient (not a generic company summary)

### Slides 4-5 — The problem / opportunity
- What is the recipient's current situation?
- What's broken / missing / inefficient?
- Quantify where possible (their data, public data, or well-sourced industry data)

### Slides 6-8 — Our solution / product
- What we're offering
- Concrete: SKUs, specs, photos, price points where relevant
- For wine: varietal + vintage + medal history + price tier + volume available
- Two-way fit: how it plugs into THEIR operation

### Slides 9-10 — Proof
- Medals / awards / reviews (full format: competition + year + level)
- Existing customers / testimonials (with permission)
- Production data (volume, vintages available)
- Press mentions (headline + outlet + date)

### Slide 11 — Commercial terms
- Pricing structure (volume tiers if applicable)
- Payment terms
- Delivery / logistics (who does what)
- Marketing support included
- Exclusivity terms if any

### Slide 12 — Timeline
- What happens next if they say yes
- Milestones: sample evaluation → pilot → full rollout
- Specific dates where possible

### Slide 13 — The ask
- Exactly what you want them to do after this meeting
- One primary + optional secondary
- Be specific: "3-month pilot listing for 2 SKUs in top-25 stores" beats "partnership"

### Slide 14 — Contact + next step
- Single point of contact
- Availability for follow-up
- Reference material link (one)

### Appendix (optional, unnumbered)
- Full SKU catalogue
- Company financials (if investor deck)
- Legal / certifications / IČO / VAT
- Supplementary case studies

## Format notes

- **One idea per slide.** If a slide needs >30 seconds to present, split it.
- **Big headers, sparse body.** Headers are sentence-case and action-oriented ("Three medals in the last 12 months" beats "Awards").
- **Visuals carry weight.** Every slide should have a reason to exist visually — photo, chart, quote card, product cutout. Not walls of text.
- **Numbers with sources.** Every stat gets a source in small type at the slide bottom.

## Bilingual (if applicable)

Usually pitch decks are monolingual per audience. If bilingual, generate TWO versions (not one mixed deck). Translated deck typically adapts layout for text expansion (e.g. German ~30% longer than English).

## Quality checklist

- Could the deck be delivered in 15-20 minutes?
- Does slide 2 earn the audience's attention in the first 30 seconds?
- Is the ask (slide 13) concrete enough to say yes or no to?
- Every number sourced?
- Every claim defensible if challenged?
- No AI-isms, em-dashes, generic intensifiers?
- Brand rules respected (e.g. ACME: never "family winery", no hectares)?
- Is this deck specific to the recipient, or could it go to any prospect?

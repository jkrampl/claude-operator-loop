# Outreach email template

## Target
Cold or warm B2B outreach. Chain buyer / distributor / press / influencer / partner / event organiser.

## Structure

1. **Subject line** (under 50 characters)
   - Reference specific context — their product, their publication, their event
   - Avoid: "Quick question", "Partnership opportunity", generic pitches
   - Good: "Račianska frankovka for [chain name] Q3 2026?", "[Publication] wine feature — gold medal winner angle"

2. **Opening** (1 sentence)
   - Name a specific thing they did / published / stock that's relevant
   - NOT "I hope this email finds you well"
   - NOT "My name is X and I represent Y"

3. **Relevance hook** (2-3 sentences)
   - Why THEM specifically, not why you
   - Tie to something public: a recent article they wrote, a range they carry, an event they organised
   - Establish credibility with one specific proof point (a medal + competition name, a ranking, a published fact)

4. **Proposition** (1-2 sentences)
   - What you're offering / asking for
   - Concrete: a sample, a co-written piece, a distribution window, a specific meeting
   - Time-bounded: "for Q3 2026", "before June", "this season"

5. **Soft CTA** (1 sentence)
   - Easy to say yes to
   - Good: "Open to a 15-minute intro call next week?"
   - Good: "Happy to send a sample to your office — confirm address?"
   - Bad: "Let me know your thoughts" (too vague)

6. **Signature**
   - Name + role + company + ONE link (website OR relevant media feature, not both)
   - Phone optional; depends on audience
   - No quotes, no "Sent from iPhone", no multi-line legal disclaimers

## Follow-up sequence (if applicable)

Default: 3 follow-ups at day 4 / day 11 / day 25.

- **Follow-up 1 (day 4):** Soft bump. Reference original subject. Add one more piece of value (new fact, clearer angle, shorter ask).
- **Follow-up 2 (day 11):** Change angle. If original was about partnership, now offer just a sample; if original was a sample, now offer an interview.
- **Follow-up 3 (day 25):** "Closing the loop" — assume no interest, thank them, leave door open. Often gets replies from people who just missed earlier emails.

## Quality checklist

- Under 150 words (cold email gold standard)?
- Specific to the recipient (could NOT be copy-pasted to anyone else)?
- One CTA only?
- No em-dashes, AI-isms, generic openers?
- If a medal is cited, is it full format (competition + year + level)?
- Any claims that could be challenged — are they sourced?
- Does the email pass the "would I reply to this" gut test?

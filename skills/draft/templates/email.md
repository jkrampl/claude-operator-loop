# Email template

## Target
Marketing or transactional email. Full email = subject + preheader + body + footer. Plain-text compatible (no HTML assumed unless specified).

## Structure

1. **Subject line** (under 50 characters for mobile preview). Tests to consider: curiosity / specific benefit / urgency / personalized.
2. **Preheader** (90 characters max, complements subject, does NOT repeat it). The "invisible second subject".
3. **Opening** (1-2 sentences) — pick up where the subject promised. Do NOT say "Hi [Name]" unless that's a project convention.
4. **Body** — 300-600 words typical for marketing; 50-150 for transactional.
   - One idea per paragraph
   - Lead with the value for the reader, not the sender's context
   - Use specifics (numbers, dates, product names)
   - Scannable: optional short bullet lists, short paragraphs
5. **Primary CTA** — one dominant action. Button text under 5 words, action-oriented, concrete (not "Learn more" / "Click here"; use "Get my discount" / "Reserve a tasting" / "Read the guide").
6. **Secondary link** (optional) — subtle, for readers not ready for the primary.
7. **Signature** — project convention (first-name signoff vs brand signoff).
8. **Footer notes** — unsubscribe link (auto-added by platform usually), physical address if required by law, preference center link.

## Bilingual (if applicable)

Two separate emails (do NOT put both languages in one — language-segment sends at the platform level). Each with its own subject + preheader optimised for that audience.

## Quality checklist

- Subject + preheader tested by reading just those two lines — does it make you want to open?
- Does the opening pay off what the subject promised?
- Is the CTA concrete and specific?
- Did you remove all AI-isms? Em-dashes? Generic phrases like "We hope this finds you well"?
- Does the body stand up to mobile scan (first 2-3 lines visible)?
- Character count per line reasonable for plain-text readers?
- Legal footer elements present per project's jurisdiction?
